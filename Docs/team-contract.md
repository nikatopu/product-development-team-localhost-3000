## Communication

**Primary channel:** Facebook Messenger group "localhost:3000 CS-PD-2026"

**Standup cadence:** Flexible, as-needed.  
We do not enforce fixed standups due to full-time work schedules. Instead, we initiate short check-ins when:

- a deadline is approaching
- progress is unclear
- coordination is required

**Response time expectation:**  
Messages should be acknowledged within:

- 3–5 hours during active hours (18:00–00:00)
- by the next morning for late-night messages

**Meeting format:**

- Online: Google Meet
- In-person: University campus (if convenient for all members)

**If someone is unresponsive:**  
If no response for 24 hours and a deadline is near:

1. A follow-up message is sent in Messenger
2. Program Lead (Giorgi) checks directly
3. If still unresolved, the issue is documented (not escalated immediately)

## Decision-Making

**Day-to-day decisions**  
(minor tools, formatting, small changes):  
Handled by any two members through discussion.

**Product decisions**  
(problem selection, feature scope, direction):  
Discussed by all three members and decided by majority.

**Major decisions / pivots**  
(significant changes in direction):

- Require a dedicated discussion (call or in-person)
- Must be agreed by at least 2 members
- Brief reasoning is written and saved in the repository

**Documentation:**  
Important decisions will be tracked in a `decision-log.md` file inside the repository.

---

## Conflict Resolution

We follow the KIU 4-Step Conflict Protocol:

**Step 1 — Verbal Discussion (15 minutes):**  
Each person explains their perspective without interruption.

**Step 2 — Written View (10 minutes):**  
Each person writes a short explanation of their position.

**Step 3 — Cool-off (24 hours):**  
No immediate decision. Time is given to reflect.

**Step 4 — Program Lead Decision:**  
If unresolved, Giorgi (Program Lead) makes the final decision.

**Escalation:**  
If the issue cannot be resolved or involves the Program Lead, the instructor may be contacted.

## Contribution and Accountability

**Tracking contributions:**

- Work is generally tracked through GitHub commits and shared documents
- Each member is responsible for committing their own work when possible

**Work expectations:**

- Contributions should be consistent, but flexible due to work schedules
- Team members communicate early if they cannot complete a task

**Deadlines:**

- Internal deadlines are treated as guidelines, not rigid constraints
- Final submission deadlines are respected strictly

**Under-contribution:**  
If a member consistently under-contributes:

- The team discusses the issue openly first
- Adjustments are made before escalation
- Peer evaluation will reflect actual contribution

---

## Team Norms

- We prioritize clarity over speed — unclear work is clarified early
- We communicate early if blocked instead of delaying silently
- We avoid over-engineering; focus is on building a working solution
- We respect each other’s time, especially given full-time jobs
- We aim to complete work before deadlines, not at the last minute

### Giorgi Tkebuchava

#### H1 -- Hairband

**Problem:**  
I (a university student) have trouble maintaining a consistent sleep schedule because I often stay up late working on assignments or browsing online and wake up early for classes, and it matters because I feel constantly tired, lose focus during lectures, and my productivity suffers.

**Where this observation comes from:**  
I personally experience this almost every day during the semester. On weekdays, I often finish assignments or read online tutorials late into the night and set alarms to wake up for 8:00 AM lectures. Even when I try to sleep earlier, distractions from my phone or course deadlines push my bedtime later. Over the past month, I have tracked that I average only 5–6 hours of sleep per night, which leaves me groggy and unfocused during classes.

**Rough severity estimate:** Medium  
The immediate consequence is reduced concentration and learning efficiency. The longer-term consequence is cumulative fatigue, which can affect mental health and overall academic performance.

#### H2 -- Headache

**Problem:**  
My classmate (who commutes daily to university by car) has trouble arriving on time because traffic jams in the city during morning hours are unpredictable, and it matters because they often miss the first part of lectures and feel stressed before the day even starts.

**Where this observation comes from:**  
I observed this firsthand over the past semester while commuting with my classmate three times per week. On multiple occasions, we left at the same time and arrived at different times due to congestion on different routes. I saw them checking traffic apps repeatedly, becoming anxious, and sometimes arriving 10–15 minutes late. Their stress and frustration were visible, and they explicitly mentioned feeling helpless because traffic conditions were beyond their control.

**Rough severity estimate:** Medium  
The consequence is missed lecture content and increased stress, which negatively affects both immediate learning and mood for the day. Over time, repeated delays can lower participation and engagement in class.

#### H3 -- Heartburn

**Problem:**  
Students at universities have trouble communicating efficiently with administration because queries about schedules, grades, and paperwork are scattered across email, forms, and in-person visits, and it matters because it causes delays, confusion, and unnecessary stress for students trying to manage their academic responsibilities.

**Where this observation comes from:**  
I have personally experienced this while trying to confirm course schedules and assignment deadlines at my university. I have also spoken to at least five other students who describe similar issues—having to send multiple emails, make repeated office visits, or wait days for a response. Administrative staff often redirect students to different platforms or forms, which leads to lost time and repeated follow-ups. This problem is consistent across multiple departments and has persisted throughout the semester.

**Rough severity estimate:** High  
The immediate consequence is wasted time and frustration. The longer-term consequence is missed deadlines, increased stress, and difficulty planning academic work efficiently, which can impact grades and overall student well-being.

#### H2 -- Headache

**Problem:**  
Metro passengers in Tbilisi have trouble knowing which station is next when traveling, especially when they rely only on audio announcements, and it matters because people can easily miss their stop or not have enough time to prepare to exit.

**Where this observation comes from:**  
I observed this both from my own experience and from other people using the Tbilisi metro. There is no clear visual indicator showing the next station, only audio announcements. If someone loses focus or does not hear the announcement clearly, they can miss their stop. This is an even bigger problem for hearing-impaired people, because the only way for them to know the station is to count stops in their head. Also, sometimes the announcement comes late, when there is almost no time left to prepare to get off.

**Rough severity estimate:** Medium  
This is medium severity because it affects many people regularly and makes public transport less accessible, but it is not a life-threatening problem.

#### H3 -- Heartburn

**Problem:**  
Backend and frontend developers have trouble communicating API details during software development, especially when new endpoints are added, and it matters because miscommunication increases development time and slows down the whole project.

**Where this observation comes from:**  
I have seen this problem many times while working as a backend developer. Whenever I created a new API endpoint, I had to manually write documentation describing the route path, request type, and response format, and then send it to the frontend developer. This process repeats very often, even for simple routes, and it wastes time. I have noticed the same problem in multiple projects, not just one.

**Rough severity estimate:** High  
This is high severity because it happens very often in software teams, and even small misunderstandings between backend and frontend developers can significantly slow down the development process.

In several work or freelancing projects I have worked with designs that only provided a desktop or a mobile version of the entire website.
